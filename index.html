About Nienke Homan

The Mind Behind Future Proof Industries

Nienke Homan (born 1979, Groningen) is an internationally recognised expert in energy transition, green hydrogen, and industrial sustainability. As Chairwoman of the Royal Association of the Dutch Chemical Industry (VNCI) and board member of the Green Hydrogen Organisation in Geneva, she has spent over a decade connecting industry, government, and science in the pursuit of a sustainable, competitive economy.

As former Regional Deputy Minister of the Province of Groningen — responsible for energy transition, climate, and water — she was the architect of the first Hydrogen Valley in the world (HEAVENN), a concept now actively promoted and replicated by the European Commission across more than 50 valleys continent-wide. She is the CEO of Impact Hydrogen and the founder of the SDGproof quality mark.

Now Nienke is bringing this proven model to the European stage: connecting industries across all 27 member states, aligning them with EU policy, and building the sustainable value chains Europe needs to lead the global transition.

The Concept

What is Future Proof Industries Europe?

Inspired by the VNCI model — which has helped make the Netherlands the fourth largest chemical producer in Europe — Future Proof Industries Europe scales this approach to the entire continent. We connect, strengthen, and represent industry across European borders to deliver a sustainable and prosperous future for all.

🔗

Connect

Building bridges between industrial associations, national governments, the European Commission, universities, and knowledge institutions across all 27 member states — creating a single, powerful European voice for industry.

💪

Strengthen

Sharing best practices, pooling expertise, and jointly advocating for smart regulatory frameworks — like the Antwerp Declaration for a European Industrial Deal — that make green transition commercially viable, not just aspirationally desirable.

🌱

Transform

Driving the shift from fossil-based to sustainable raw materials and energy across chemical clusters, manufacturing sectors, and regional supply chains — ensuring the transition is fair, innovative, and anchored in real industrial reality.

Proven in the Netherlands,
Ready for Europe

The VNCI model demonstrates what is possible: a €64 billion sector, 48,000 jobs, 415+ companies, and the fourth-largest chemical producer in Europe — all working together through coordinated representation, shared knowledge, and a commitment to safety, sustainability, and innovation. Future Proof Industries Europe replicates this success at continental scale, serving industries beyond chemistry across all sectors of European manufacturing.

Our Vision

A Sustainable, Competitive Industrial Europe

Europe can only decarbonise its industry and keep it here if we actively create demand for sustainable products, build robust value chains, and ensure that regulation enables rather than hinders the transition.

Hydrogen Valleys across Europe: Scale the world's first Hydrogen Valley model (HEAVENN, Northern Netherlands) to 50+ valleys across Europe by 2030 — as committed by the European Commission — creating regional green industrial hubs.

Cross-sector decarbonisation: Chemistry, ceramics, steel, food, and materials manufacturing working in concert — sharing circular technologies, biobased innovation, and electrification pathways.

Fair and inclusive transition: Ensuring that the green transition creates new prosperity for regions, workers, and communities — not just for capital. A green economy only succeeds when everyone participates.

Smart European regulation: Advocating for demand-side policies, not just supply-side rules — creating the market pull needed for sustainable products to become commercially viable at scale across Europe.

5. Your GDPR Rights

⏸️ Right to Restriction (Art. 18)

You have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances, for example while a complaint is being investigated.

📦 Right to Data Portability (Art. 20)

🚫 Right to Object (Art. 21)

↩️ Right to Withdraw Consent (Art. 7)

⚖️ Right to Lodge a Complaint (Art. 77)
